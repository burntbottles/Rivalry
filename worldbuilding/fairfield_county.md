# FAIRFIELD COUNTY WORLDBUILDING

## GEOGRAPHY & LOCATIONS

### Fairfield County, Connecticut
**Time Period:** Contemporary (modern day with a timeless feel)  
**Climate:** Temperate, Four seasons (story begins in early autumn)  
**Population:** Mix of suburban homes, natural forest areas, protected lands  

---

## KEY LOCATIONS

### 1. SQUIRREL TERRITORY - DAYTIME DOMAIN
**Primary Location:** Ancient Oak Forest (East side of town)

**Description:**
- Large, mature oak and maple trees
- Well-maintained, organized feel
- Treehouses and tree-forts in various oaks
- Squirrel Headquarters: A carved-out tree-fort with military aesthetic
- Training grounds for the Defense Force
- Abundant acorn sources (seasonal)

**Characteristics:**
- Bright and sunny during the day
- Organized supply chains for acorns
- Bird feeders (strategic resources)
- Clean, structured society

**Time Rule:** Squirrels are active from sunrise to sunset

---

### 2. RACCOON TERRITORY - NIGHTTIME DOMAIN
**Primary Location:** Downtown/Urban Area (West side of town)

**Description:**
- Mix of residential neighborhoods and wooded areas
- Storm drains and underground passages
- Garbage cans and refuse areas (resource centers)
- Raccoon Headquarters: Converted storm drain system
- Organized chaos - maps mark good foraging locations
- Trash management centers

**Characteristics:**
- Active primarily after sunset
- Dim lighting, shadowy streets
- Underground tunnel systems
- Resourceful, adaptive society

**Time Rule:** Raccoons are active from sunset to sunrise

---

### 3. NEUTRAL ZONES

#### Town Square
- Center of Fairfield County
- Town Hall with Mayor Hoot's office
- Meeting point between both species
- Generally accepted as neutral ground

#### The Border Forest
- Transitional area between day and night territories
- Mixed daylight and shadow throughout the day
- Historical meeting ground for treaties
- Where most inter-species diplomacy occurs

#### Sunset Clearing
- Featured in pilot episode
- Natural gathering point where day and night meet equally
- Emotionally significant location for species unity

---

### 4. THE ANCIENT FOREST
**Location:** Deep woods beyond town boundaries  
**Significance:** Where "The Shadow" dwells  
**Characteristics:**
- Old growth forest, primordial feeling
- Rarely visited by regular creatures
- Contains mysterious markings and ancient trees
- Source of fear and mystery

---

## SOCIETY & CULTURE

### SQUIRREL SOCIETY

**Government:** Military hierarchy with Arnold as Defense Force Commander

**Economy:** Acorn-based (primary resource)
- Acorn storage and preservation
- Trade with other species for winter supplies
- Seasonal stockpiling

**Values:**
- Order and preparation
- Daytime activity and visibility
- Collective defense
- Long-term planning

**Traditions:**
- Autumn acorn harvest celebrations
- Defense drills and training
- Tree-marking ceremonies
- Honor codes within the force

**Daily Life:**
- Training and defense preparation
- Acorn gathering and storage
- Community maintenance
- Sentry watches at territory borders

---

### RACCOON SOCIETY

**Government:** Hierarchical with Sylvester as Commander of the Trash Guard

**Economy:** Scavenging and trading
- Trash as primary resource
- Bartering system for goods
- Nighttime foraging operations
- Resource mapping and intelligence

**Values:**
- Resourcefulness and adaptation
- Nighttime autonomy
- Collective problem-solving
- Innovation and creativity

**Traditions:**
- Nighttime patrols and expeditions
- Trash sorting ceremonies (ironic humor intended)
- Engineering innovation contests
- Honor codes within the guard

**Daily Life:**
- Rest during day (reverse schedule from squirrels)
- Nighttime patrols and resource gathering
- Engineering and building projects
- Territory defense and lookout duties

## SPECIES CHARACTERISTICS

### SQUIRRELS
**Physical Attributes:**
- High metabolism (active during day)
- Excellent climbing and jumping
- Sharp claws for tree work
- Bushy, expressive tails
- Excellent daytime vision

**Skills:**
- Tree navigation
- Acorn processing
- Organization
- Long-term planning

**Weaknesses:**
- Vulnerable at night
- Limited night vision

- Dependent on trees

---

### RACCOONS
**Physical Attributes:**
- Nocturnal adaptations (excellent night vision)
- Dexterous paws (tool users)
- Masked faces (intimidating when angry)
- Excellent swimmers
- Strong for their size

**Skills:**
- Tool use and manipulation
- Problem-solving
- Survival in various environments
- Quick learning and adaptation

**Weaknesses:**
- Vulnerable during the day
- Limited daytime vision

## THE ANCIENT AGREEMENT

### Historical Context
Before the pilot episode, squirrels and raccoons had established an uneasy peace based on:

1. **Temporal Division:** 
   - Squirrels: Daytime territory and activity
   - Raccoons: Nighttime territory and activity
   - Minimal overlap reduced conflict

2. **Resource Separation:**
   - Squirrels: Trees, acorns, bird feeders
   - Raccoons: Ground-level, garbage, urban areas
   - Limited resource competition

3. **Mutual Non-Interference:**
   - Neither species intrudes on the other's "hours"
   - Border agreements respected
   - Mayor Hoot as neutral arbiter

### The Problem with the Agreement
- Requires constant separation
- Based on fear of conflict, not genuine peace
- Creates social distance and misunderstanding
- Unsustainable as populations grow
- Vulnerable to outside threats (like "The Shadow")

## TECHNOLOGY LEVEL

### Squirrel Technology
- Natural materials (wood, acorns)
- Simple mechanical devices (acorn launchers, slingshots)
- Storage and preservation systems
- Tree-based architecture
- Maps and organizational systems
- **Level:** Pre-industrial, nature-based

### Raccoon Technology
- Scavenged and repurposed materials
- Tool creation from found objects
- Mechanical improvisation
- Gadgets and contraptions
- Engineering and problem-solving
- **Level:** Scrap-based, innovative

### Combined Potential
- When working together, create more sophisticated devices
- Cross-species innovation leads to breakthroughs
- Theme of "unity creates strength"

## SEASONAL CYCLES

### Autumn (Story Starting Point)
- **Squirrels:** Acorn harvest at peak
- **Raccoons:** Preparing for winter with extra foraging
- **Creatures:** Both species busy with seasonal tasks
- **Weather:** Mild, colorful leaves, cool nights
- **The Shadow:** Awakens during this time

### Winter (Potential Story Arc)
- **Squirrels:** Indoor communities, living off acorn storage
- **Raccoons:** Limited activity, denning behavior
- **Creatures:** Most vulnerable; unified defense necessary
- **The Shadow:** Most active during winter months (mystery to be revealed)

### Spring & Summer
- **Squirrels:** Raising young, rebuilding defenses
- **Raccoons:** Returning to night activities, establishing new dens
- **Creatures:** Recovery and relationship-building
- **The Shadow:** Dormant or in retreat

## FAIRFIELD COUNTY HUMAN ELEMENT

**Important Note:** Humans are largely background/off-screen
- They provide the suburban setting
- Human houses, yards, streets are present but humans rarely seen
- Creatures operate around human civilization
- Human pets (dogs, cats) exist but typically avoid the main conflicts
- Adds to the "hidden world" feel of the story

---

## MYSTERY OF THE SHADOW

**What We Know:**
- Ancient presence in Fairfield County
- Has been dormant for generations
- Mayor Hoot knows of its existence
- Awakens when "balance is broken"
- Much larger than local creatures
- Golden glowing eyes
- Primordial roar

**What We Don't Know:**
- What is it specifically?
- Why is it waking up now?
- What does it want?
- How do you defeat it?
- What will its revelation mean?

**Thematic Purpose:**
- External threat forces internal cooperation
- Fear of the unknown can unite enemies
- Mystery drives the season's narrative arc

---

## WORLD RULES & PHYSICS

1. **Temporal Separation:** Until the pilot, squirrels and raccoons rarely meet
2. **No Speech From Others:** Only these two species talk; others are background (Mayor Hoot is exception as owl-judge)
3. **Realistic Animal Behavior Base:** Modern cartooning with expressive faces/emotions
4. **No Human Communication:** Creatures keep their activities hidden
5. **Natural Consequences:** Actions have realistic (for animated) results
6. **Magic/Supernatural:** Only "The Shadow" is mysterious/supernatural; everything else is grounded

---

**WORLDBUILDING PRINCIPLES:**
- Create a believable world that feels lived-in
- Both species should feel justified in their views
- Setting should reinforce themes of division and unity
- Mystery of "The Shadow" drives all story forward
